<p><ul><li>Inner Rotor Motor</li><li>Outer Rotor Motor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electronically Commutated (EC) motors can be categorized into two main types: inner rotor motors and outer rotor motors. Inner rotor motors feature a rotor located inside the stator, providing a compact design and higher rotational speeds, making them ideal for applications like fans and pumps. In contrast, outer rotor motors have the rotor on the outside, allowing for a larger surface area and improved cooling, which is beneficial for applications requiring high torque and efficiency, such as electric bikes and HVAC systems.</p></p>
