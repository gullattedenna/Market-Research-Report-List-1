<p><ul><li>Horizontal</li><li>Vertical</li><li>Flat</li><li>Continuous</li><li>Oscillating</li><li>Bent</li><li>Uni-directional</li><li>Bi-directional</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The glass tempering systems market comprises various types designed for specific applications. Horizontal and vertical systems refer to the orientation during tempering, with horizontal allowing for larger glass panels. Flat systems focus on producing flat glass, while continuous systems enable uninterrupted processing for high volumes. Oscillating systems move glass in a back-and-forth manner for even heating. Bent systems accommodate curved glass, while uni-directional and bi-directional systems define the glass movement direction during the tempering process, influencing efficiency and design flexibility.</p></p>
