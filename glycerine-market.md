<p><p>The glycerine market is primarily divided into two types: crude and refined glycerine. Crude glycerine is a byproduct of the biodiesel production process and contains impurities, making it suitable for industries like animal feed and chemical manufacturing. In contrast, refined glycerine undergoes processing to remove impurities and achieve higher purity levels, making it ideal for applications in pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and food. Both markets serve different industrial needs, with refined glycerine commanding a higher price due to its quality.</p></p>
