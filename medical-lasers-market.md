<p><p>The medical lasers market comprises various laser types, each suited for specific applications. Solid-state laser systems use crystalline materials for efficient output, commonly employed in dermatology and surgery. Gas laser systems utilize gases like carbon dioxide and argon, ideal for cutting and coagulating tissue. Dye laser systems, leveraging organic dyes, enable versatile wavelength adjustments for treating conditions like vascular lesions. Diode laser systems, compact and energy-efficient, are preferred in applications like hair removal and dental procedures due to their precision.</p></p>
