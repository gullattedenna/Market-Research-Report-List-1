<p><p>The olefinic thermoplastic elastomers market primarily comprises two types: thermoplastic polyolefins (TPO) and thermoplastic vulcanizates (TPV). TPOs are versatile materials that combine the properties of rubber and plastic, offering excellent flexibility, chemical resistance, and durability, making them suitable for automotive and construction applications. TPVs, on the other hand, are created by dynamically vulcanizing rubber within a thermoplastic matrix, providing superior heat resistance and mechanical properties, ideal for automotive seals and gaskets. Both types are increasingly favored for their sustainability and recyclability.</p></p>
