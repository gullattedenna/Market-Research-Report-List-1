<p><p>The Serum Freezing Culture Media market encompasses two primary types: DMSO-based and DMSO-free formulations. DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide) is commonly used as a cryoprotectant to prevent ice crystal formation during cell freezing, enhancing cell viability upon thawing. In contrast, DMSO-free media cater to those seeking alternatives due to concerns about potential toxicity or contamination. These formulations are designed to maintain cell integrity and functionality during cryopreservation without the use of DMSO, broadening options for researchers in cell biology and regenerative medicine.</p></p>
