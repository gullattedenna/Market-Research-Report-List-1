<p><p>The table tennis racquets market is primarily divided into two grip types: Penhold and Shakehand. The Penhold grip, popularized in Asia, involves holding the racket like holding a pen, allowing for quick wrist movements and control during gameplay. In contrast, the Shakehand grip resembles a handshake, providing versatility and stability, favored by many Western players. Both grip styles cater to different playing techniques and strategies, influencing player performance and preferences within the competitive table tennis landscape.</p></p>
